# Quantified Wins Bank

A flat list of every measurable achievement from your career. Tagged so an AI can grep by skill/role/tech and pull what matches a JD.

**Format:** One bullet per win. Lead with the metric. Tag aggressively — every win gets skill tags, a role tag, and a year tag.

**Why this is separate from the Career Spine:** The spine tells a *narrative*; this bank is a *search index*. Both are useful for different reasons.

**Rules that keep this bank trustworthy:**

- **Every win must be real and user-grounded.** Never keep fabricated "format demo" examples in a live bank — they are a hallucination risk for every future generation session. (The fictional examples below exist only in this template; delete them when you fill it.)
- **Verify mined numbers.** JD-tailored resumes inflate. When a win comes from an old resume, cross-check it against performance reviews or project notes, and mark it verified.
- **Canonical phrasing beats legacy phrasing.** When a win has ever been stated misleadingly (e.g. a percentage that doesn't survive interview math), record the CANONICAL RESUME PHRASING on the win itself and ban the legacy form explicitly — so no session resurrects it.
- **Attribution locks.** If a metric belongs to one employer/era and could be misfiled under another, pin it on the win: "attribute to [Role A], never [Role B]."
- **Defensible framings.** Some wins (especially tooling-related) have multiple honest framings depending on the JD — same achievement, different tool emphasis. Document the allowed framings inline so AI doesn't have to guess.

## Mining old resumes into this bank

If you have an archive of historical resume versions, mine it **once**:

1. Open a resume.
2. Find any bullet starting with or containing a number.
3. Paste it here in the standard format above.
4. Add tags by reading the role context.
5. Move on. Don't get caught up rewriting — capture first, polish later.
6. When the archive is fully mined, record that in `HISTORY.md` so no future session re-reads the binaries.

Target: 50-100 wins covering your full career. That's enough that any JD will find 4-8 matches.
