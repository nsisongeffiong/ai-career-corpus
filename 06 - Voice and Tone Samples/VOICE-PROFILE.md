# Voice Profile — Authoritative (template)

Derive this file from the user-authored samples in this folder, then have the user validate it. When generating cover letters, **this profile overrides general style instincts, any literary-writing skill's register, and any urge to be clever.**

An AI's two failure modes when imitating a professional voice are opposite and equally wrong: corporate puff ("results-driven professional passionate about...") and literary showmanship (aphoristic hooks, scene-setting, essayistic rhythm). The profile's job is to pin down where between those poles the user's actual register sits.

## Sections to fill

### The register (one line)

*[e.g. "Measured professional warmth — plain, direct, conventionally professional; not literary, not punchy."]*

### Observed features

Derive each from the samples, with a quoted example pattern:

- **How openers work.** *[e.g. "Leads with the differentiator, stated plainly — a positioning clause does the work, not a clever hook. Never open with an aphorism, a scene, or an industry observation."]*
- **How credit is shared.** *[e.g. "Collaborative credit: 'helped drive', 'led and supported' — never solo-hero framing, even for solo wins."]*
- **Sentence shapes.** *[e.g. "Subject-verb-object. One idea per sentence. No colon-cascades, no rhetorical triads, no fragment-for-effect. Zero em dashes (see `09 - Resume Style Spec.md` — AI tell)."]*
- **How motivation is stated.** *[e.g. "Directly: 'This role appeals to me because it focuses on X, Y, Z.' Say why; don't dramatize it."]*
- **Confidence level.** *[e.g. "Modest confidence — claims concrete but understated; the proof carries the weight, not the verbs."]*
- **The close.** *[e.g. "Warm, simple: 'I would welcome the opportunity to bring [specifics] to [team]. Thank you for your consideration.'"]*

### Ban list

Empty filler is banned regardless of register: "proven track record", "passionate about", "dynamic", "results-driven". Add the user's own additions. Note which *conventional* phrases the user actually uses and accepts (e.g. "I am excited to apply" may be fine if it appears in their own writing) — the enemy is emptiness and affectation, in both directions.

### Interaction with writing-craft skills

If a general writing/editing skill is available, use it for **clarity only**: cut adverbs, prefer concrete nouns, delete throat-clearing. Do NOT use it for register.

### Quick test before showing a draft

Read the opener aloud. If it sounds like the first line of an essay or a TED talk, rewrite it as a plain statement of who the candidate is and why they fit. If a sentence would make a writing teacher smile, simplify it until it would merely make a hiring manager nod.
