# Voice and Tone Samples

> ⚠️ **TONE-ONLY WARNING:** The documents in this folder are voice/register references ONLY. **Facts, metrics, dates, and claims inside them are NON-CANONICAL** — samples often predate corpus corrections and may contain since-banned claims or phrasings. Never reuse a fact or figure from a voice sample; all facts come from the Spine and Wins Bank. Read `VOICE-PROFILE.md` first — it is the authoritative voice guidance, derived from these samples.

When an AI generates resume bullets, cover letters, or LinkedIn posts, it defaults to bland corporate-speak — samples of your own writing plus the distilled profile fix that. **Never add AI-drafted documents here**; samples must be the user's own writing, or the profile starts imitating the AI imitating you.

The sneaky case: a cover letter this very system generated, which you liked, sent, and maybe got an interview from. It *feels* like yours now. It is not a voice sample — it's the AI's approximation of your voice, and feeding it back in compounds the drift with every generation. Only pre-AI writing, or prose you composed yourself from scratch, qualifies.

## What belongs here

- 2-3 of your favorite blog posts, essays, or LinkedIn write-ups
- A particularly well-written cover letter you wrote (not generic — one where the words feel like yours)
- A personal statement you're proud of
- Any short prose where someone might say *"That sounds like you."*

## What does NOT belong here

- Generic resumes (formal, list-style — too structured to reflect voice)
- Anything written by someone else
- Anything AI-drafted
- Stuff that's *fine* but not *you*

## How to use it

Once 2-3 samples are here, have an AI derive `VOICE-PROFILE.md` from them (a template is provided), then **review and correct it** — the profile is only authoritative after you've signed off on it. From then on, generation sessions read the profile, not the raw samples.
